In Dominaria United (DSK) Limited, archetypes tend to follow a "rock-paper-scissors" structure where different decks counter or struggle against others based on their core strategies, strengths, and weaknesses. Aggro decks (e.g., Rakdos, Boros) aim to pressure opponents quickly with early creatures and combat tricks. These decks are strong against slower, value-driven decks like Control (Dimir, Orzhov) because control decks often need time to stabilize. However, aggro can struggle against Midrange (Gruul, Selesnya) strategies that pack strong creatures, allowing midrange decks to pivot from defense to offense and stabilize the game. Midrange decks excel at using efficient creatures and removal to dominate the battlefield over time, which makes them well-suited to defeating aggro decks by controlling the board and outclassing smaller threats. However, midrange can have a tough time against Tempo (Azorius, Izzet) decks that leverage disruption and evasive threats. Tempo decks often get ahead with early aggression while simultaneously preventing midrange from developing its board effectively. Control decks tend to thrive in late-game scenarios, where they can overpower aggro's early pushes with efficient removal and card advantage. However, control decks are vulnerable to Ramp (Simic) or graveyard strategies (Golgari), which can deploy big threats or recursive engines that eventually overwhelm the slower, reactive control game plan. Tempo decks sit somewhere between aggro and control, focusing on early threats and disruption. These decks excel against midrange strategies but may struggle against faster, aggressive decks that don’t give them time to set up, or slower control decks that can outlast their threats. Meanwhile, decks with a solid long game or ramping into big plays will be a tough matchup for tempo if they can survive the early pressure. This structure encourages diversity and adaptation, where understanding your deck’s place in this dynamic and anticipating opponents’ strategies is key to success.
